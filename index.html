Why millimeters matter

Higher water changes the baseline

Sea-level rise compounds everyday coastal pressures and lets storm tides reach farther inland.

01

Flooding and erosion

Higher starting water levels make nuisance flooding more frequent and accelerate the loss of beaches and coastal land.

02

Fresh water and food

Saltwater can enter groundwater, soils, and wetlands, putting drinking water and low-lying crops under pressure.

03

Homes and services

Roads, ports, power, health facilities, and homes face more frequent damage, disruption, and costly adaptation.

04

Movement and ecosystems

Repeated losses can contribute to displacement while reefs, mangroves, and coastal habitats struggle to keep pace.

Read the lines carefully

Two records, two questions

Satellite-era series: annual values from 1993 to 2023 are available for all 21 places. The headline change compares the 2019–2023 average with the 1993–1997 average, reducing the influence of any single year.

Historical series: annual tide-gauge anomalies are relative to each station’s 1993–2000 mean and then averaged to country level. Coverage changes through time, and missing years are shown as gaps.

The regional lines are equal-country means for each year. They do not weight countries by coastline, land area, population, or number of tide-gauge stations.
