All information about the challenge can be found here (rules, dataset, etc): https://pacificdatavizchallenge.org/

Participants in the Pacific Dataviz Challenge need to use at least one dataset from the official list which changes for each year’s competition with the theme.

Participants are also free to incorporate other datasets from the Pacific Data Hub, or any other source of open data. Participants in the Pacific schools’ competition can also use their own primary data—for example, data collected as part of a school project.

All data sources will need to be listed in the submission form.

Rules

    Your dataviz must use at least one dataset from the official list.

    Additional datasets can be used so long as they are accessible as open data. Participants in the Pacific schools’ competition can also use their own primary data.

    Your dataviz must be made public.

    Anyone of any age can participate.

    Anyone from any country or territory can participate.

    You are free to use any data visualisation tools of your choice.

    Participation in the challenge is free of charge.

Official data can be found here: https://pacificdata.org/ and here: https://stats.pacificdata.org/
